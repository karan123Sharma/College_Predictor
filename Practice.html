Top Engineering Colleges in India 2021: Ranking, Fees, Admission

IITs, NITS and IIITs are among the top engineering colleges in India. There are nearly 4,200 engineering colleges in India that offer BTech courses, out of these nearly 2,100 colleges are private, nearly 450 are government and the rest are public-private colleges. Engineering is a stream of education that involves the application of Science, Technology and Mathematics to innovate, design, develop and maintain machines, structures, software, hardware and systems & processes. This field provides a range of career opportunities across all industries, making it one of the most sought-after courses pursued by students after Class 12, especially those from the Science stream. The BTech programme is your gateway to a career in Engineering with a duration of four years.

The top Engineering colleges in India accept scores obtained by students in numerous national or state-level examinations, including JEE Main, TNEA, MHT CET, AP EAMCET, UPSEE and many more. There are several BTech colleges in major cities across the country such as Delhi/ NCR, Chennai, Hyderabad, Kolkata, Mumbai, Bengaluru, Pune and so on.

Engineering Colleges in India accepting JEE Main: Eligibility Criteria

The eligibility criteria for the BTech courses may differ from college to college. However, the basic eligibility criteria for BTech is as follows. The candidates:

  • Must have passed Class 12 with a minimum of 60% aggregate from a recognised board with Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics as core subjects (the pass percentage may vary in different colleges)
  • Admission is based on merit, written exam, Group Discussion or Personal Interview and it varies from college to college

Agencies like NIRF give ranking to colleges based on various parameters like teaching, learning and resources, research and professional practices, graduation outcomes, outreach and inclusivity and perception. Shiksha aggregate rating is an authenticated rating given by the students based on five different parameters such as placements, infrastructure, faculty & course, curriculum, crowd & campus life and value for money.

ROI of Top Engineering Colleges in India accepting JEE Main

Return on Investment or ROI shows how beneficial it is to pursue a particular course from a particular college. It is the comparison between the fee invested by the student while studying and the return he/she gets in the form of placement. In the case of popular engineering courses, selecting a college or university based on the ROI factor can be very much valued. This is helpful when it becomes difficult to choose between two shortlisted colleges.

FAQs

Q1. What are the different specialisations offered in Engineering colleges in India?

A. The different specialisations offered in Engineering colleges in India are:

·         Computer Science Engineering

·         Civil Engineering

·         Electrical Engineering

·         Electronics and Communication Engineering

·         Chemical Engineering

·         Electronics Engineering

·         Information Technology

·         Mechanical Engineering

·         Aeronautical Engineering

·         Biotechnology Engineering

Q2. Are there any job opportunities for students who pursue Engineering Courses?

A. Yes, there are several job opportunities for Engineering students. Some of them are listed below:

·         Professor
·         Information Technology Officer
·         Engineering Support Specialist
·         Quality EngineerSoftware Engineer
·         Software Engineer
·         Mechanical Engineer
·         Design Engineer

Q3. What is the estimated fee for studying Engineering courses in India?

A. It is estimated that pursuing Engineering Courses in India is very expensive as 58% of the colleges have a course/annual fee of more than Rs 5 lakh. Some of these colleges are:

·         IIT Madras

·         IIT Delhi

·         IIT Bombay

·         IIT Kanpur

·         IIT Kharagpur

·         IIT Roorkee

·         IIT Guwahati

·         IIT Hyderabad 

·         National Institute of Technology, Tiruchirappalli

Q4. What are the popular exams to qualify for Engineering courses in India?

A. The various exams accepted in India for Engineering courses admissions include JEE Main, JEE Advanced, TNEA, KCET, MHT CET, TS EAMCET, COME DK UGET, AP EAMCET, WBJEE, GUJCET, etc.

Q5.What are the job opportunities after I complete a Private Engineering course from India?

A. Yes, there are several job opportunities for students who complete a Private Engineering course from India. Some of them are listed below:

  • Aeronautical Engineer
  • Industrial Engineer
  • Aerospace Engineer
  • Marine Engineer
  • Automobile Engineer
  • Mechanical Engineer
  • Biomedical Engineer
  • Mechatronics Engineer
  • Biotechnology Engineer
  • Metallurgical Engineer
